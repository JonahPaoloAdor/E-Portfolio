Challenging Firm Independence: Enhancing Bankruptcy Prediction with Industry-Adjusted Cox Proportional Hazards Models

Undergraduate Thesis · De La Salle University · 2024–2025

This study challenges a core assumption of one of the most widely used bankruptcy prediction tools in finance — the Cox Proportional Hazards Model (CPHM) — which treats firms as independent from one another. This research demonstrates that firms within the same industry share common risk exposures, making their financial distress events correlated, not independent.

Using data from publicly listed U.S. firms from 1999 to 2019, the industry-adjusted model materially outperformed the standard approach:

0.630 Standard Model (C-index)
0.708 Industry-Adjusted Model (C-index)

This finding has direct relevance to audit risk assessment, going-concern evaluations, and financial advisory — areas where accurate distress prediction protects investors, creditors, and the public interest.

Personal Values & Professional Philosophy

I entered these professions believing that technical excellence is necessary but not sufficient. A capable professional must also be principled.

01

Integrity & Independence

Audit exists because markets need trust. I take seriously the auditor's obligation to independence — not merely as a regulatory requirement, but as a moral commitment. When I evaluate evidence, I do so with the intent to report what is true, not what is convenient.

02

Analytical Courage

My thesis challenged a dominant assumption in bankruptcy prediction methodology — not out of contrarianism, but because the evidence warranted a different conclusion. Rigorous thinking requires the willingness to question established frameworks and propose alternatives grounded in data.

03

Service & the Common Good

As a Lasallian, I was formed in the tradition of service. In my career, this translates into work that protects the public interest — audits that prevent fraud, financial models that support sound decisions, and advisory work that creates sustainable value for all stakeholders.
